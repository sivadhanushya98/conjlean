\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{ConjLean}, an end-to-end system for automated mathematical
conjecture generation, symbolic pre-filtering via SymPy, Lean~4 formalization,
and four-layer proof search, paired with \textbf{REFUTE}, a multi-agent
counterexample-search system.
REFUTE coordinates three agents: an R-Agent applying four search strategies
(boundary sweep, random structured sampling, symbolic perturbation, and
analogical transfer), an S-Agent selecting strategies via UCB1 bandit, and
a C-Agent that proposes refined conjectures on counterexample discovery.
On a 92-conjecture benchmark spanning three difficulty tiers and three
mathematical domains, REFUTE with Qwen2.5-Math-7B achieves precision $1.000$,
recall $0.292$ ($95\%$ CI $[0.19, 0.39]$), and F$_1$ $0.452$ ($[0.32, 0.57]$)
with zero false positives.
Fixing a SymPy NL-parser bug raises recall to $0.389$ ($[0.28, 0.50]$),
recovering 7 additional refutations; scaling to Qwen2.5-32B (NF4 int4) leaves
all metrics unchanged.
All refutations arise from the deterministic BOUNDARY strategy; large language
model (LLM)-dependent strategies contribute no verified counterexamples at any
tested configuration.
\end{abstract}
