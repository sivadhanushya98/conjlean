\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{ConjLean}, an end-to-end pipeline for automated mathematical
conjecture generation, SymPy symbolic pre-filtering, Lean~4 formalization, and
four-layer escalating proof search, paired with \textbf{REFUTE}, a multi-agent
counterexample-search system.
REFUTE coordinates an R-Agent (four search strategies: boundary sweep,
random structured sampling, symbolic perturbation, and analogical transfer),
an S-Agent that selects strategies via a UCB1 bandit over cross-conjecture
statistics, and a C-Agent that proposes refined conjectures when a
counterexample is confirmed.
We evaluate on a 92-conjecture benchmark spanning three difficulty tiers and
three mathematical domains, with all ground-truth labels verified
programmatically.
Using Qwen2.5-Math-7B-Instruct, REFUTE achieves precision~$1.000$,
recall~$0.292$ ($95\%$\,CI $[0.19,\,0.39]$), and F$_1$~$0.452$
($[0.32,\,0.57]$) with zero false positives.
Scaling to Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct (NF4 int4) does not improve recall within
this experimental setting, indicating the current bottleneck is not resolved
by this scale increase alone; LLM-dependent strategies produced no verified
counterexamples at either scale.
\end{abstract}
