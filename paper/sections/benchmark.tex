\section{Benchmark}
\label{sec:benchmark}

We construct a benchmark of 92 conjectures across three tiers and three
mathematical domains (number theory, inequality, combinatorics).
All Tier~1 and Tier~2 ground-truth labels were verified programmatically
before inclusion; no label was assigned by LLM judgment alone.

\paragraph{Tier 1 — Synthetic-false (59 conjectures).}
Conjectures are designed to be false with a small, verifiable counterexample.
All 59 have a confirmed SymPy-verified counterexample.
This tier tests basic refutation capacity.

\paragraph{Tier 2 — Historical (23 conjectures).}
Conjectures drawn from the history of mathematics with known resolution,
including the Euler sum-of-powers conjecture, the Mertens conjecture,
Fermat's Last Theorem (encoded as finite-range instances), and
Goldbach-adjacent statements.
6 of the 23 are false (verified); 17 are true.

\paragraph{Tier 3 — Subtle / open (10 conjectures).}
Cases involving scope ambiguity, near-tautologies, or open problems.
7 of the 10 are classified as false under a strict reading; 3 are true.
This tier tests specificity and false-positive resistance.
