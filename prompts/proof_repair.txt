## How to Interpret Common Lean Errors

### `unknown identifier '<name>'`
- The identifier does not exist in the current scope or Mathlib.
- Fix: check the correct Mathlib lemma name (e.g. `Nat.dvd_add` not `dvd_add_nat`).
- Alternatives: `exact?`, `simp [*]`, or reformulate the step.

### `type mismatch` / `expected type`
- The term you supplied has the wrong type.
- Fix: check whether you need a coercion (`(↑n : ℤ)`) or `push_cast` to align types.
- For Nat/Int mismatches: add `push_cast; ` before arithmetic tactics.

### `failed to synthesize instance`
- A typeclass instance is missing (e.g. `DecidableEq`, `Fintype`).
- Fix: add `haveI : Fintype ... := ...` or use a different formulation that does not require the missing instance.

### `application type mismatch` (wrong number/type of arguments)
- You passed too many, too few, or wrong-typed arguments to a lemma.
- Fix: consult the lemma's signature via `#check Lemma.name` and adjust.

### `tactic 'omega' failed` / `linarith failed`
- The arithmetic claim may be nonlinear or involve type coercions.
- Fix:
  - For nonlinear: switch to `nlinarith [sq_nonneg ...]` with algebraic hints.
  - For coercion issues: use `push_cast; omega` or `norm_cast; omega`.
  - For Nat subtraction: remember `n - k` in Nat is saturated; use `Int` or rewrite goal.

### `ring failed` / `ring_nf failed`
- The equality is not a ring identity.
- Fix: check if you need `field_simp` first, or if the goal has side conditions that need `have` steps.

### `simp made no progress`
- `simp` could not simplify the goal.
- Fix: provide explicit lemma hints: `simp [Nat.add_comm, h]` or switch to `ring` / `omega`.

### `goals remaining` after `simp` / `omega`
- The tactic did not close all goals; subgoals are left open.
- Fix: split with `constructor` / `And.intro` and prove each branch, or add `<;> omega` after pattern matching.

### `decide tactic failed` / `kernel rejected proof`
- The proposition is not decidable or the universe is too large.
- Fix: switch to a non-`decide` approach such as `norm_num`, `omega`, or a manual proof.

## Repair Strategies (try in order)

1. **Address the exact error** — fix only what the error message identifies.
2. **Simplify the approach** — if the previous attempt was complex, try a simpler tactic first (e.g. `omega`, `norm_num`, `decide`).
3. **Add coercions / casts** — if there are type mismatches between ℕ and ℤ, prepend `push_cast` or `norm_cast`.
4. **Add `have` steps** — break the goal into proved intermediate lemmas before the final close.
5. **Use `nlinarith` with hints** — for nonlinear arithmetic, add `[sq_nonneg (a - b), mul_self_nonneg a]` etc.
6. **Fallback to `aesop`** — if the goal is structurally complex and domain-general.
